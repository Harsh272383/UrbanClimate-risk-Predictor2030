About the Project

The Urban Climate Risk Predictor 2030 is an advanced machine learning tool designed to help urban planners, policymakers, and citizens understand and prepare for climate-related risks in the near future.

Project Overview

The Urban Climate Risk Predictor 2030 uses machine learning to forecast climate-related risks in urban areas based on various environmental factors. This innovative tool helps identify potential vulnerabilities and suggest adaptive strategies to mitigate climate-related challenges.

Key Features:
  • Predictive Analysis: Utilizes random forest algorithms to predict climate risk levels
  • Customized Recommendations: Generates tailored adaptation strategies based on specific risk factors
  • Interactive Visualizations: Presents risk data through intuitive charts and graphs
  • Future Projections: Models climate conditions in 2030 based on current trends
  • Multi-factor Analysis: Considers temperature, precipitation, air quality, and other critical factors

The Machine Learning Model

Our model employs an ensemble learning approach using Random Forest classification to determine risk levels. The algorithm analyzes patterns in historical climate data and identifies factor combinations that lead to different risk scenarios.

Data Sources:

The model is trained on a comprehensive dataset that includes:

  • Historical temperature and precipitation patterns
  • Air quality measurements
  • Geographical and topographical information
  • Urban development patterns
  • Green space distribution
Model Performance:

The current model achieves an accuracy of over 85% in predicting climate risk levels, based on validation against historical data and expert assessments.
